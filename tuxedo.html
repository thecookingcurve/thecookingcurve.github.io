Chocolate Tuxedo Cake

A perfect birthday cake! Dark chocolate cake with layers of cream cheese frosting and whipped chocolate ganache.

Serves 5

INGREDIENTS

CHOCOLATE CAKE

- 2 cups all purpose flour

- 1/2 cup vegetable oil (any unflavoured oil works)

- 1 and 1/2 cup white sugar

- 2/3 cup cocoa powder

- 1 cup whole milk

- 1 cup hot strong coffee

- 2 eggs

- 1 tsp almond extract

- 1 tsp vanilla extract

- 1 and 1/2 tsp baking soda

- 2 tsp baking powder

- 1 tsp salt

CREAM CHEESE FROSTING

- 1/2 cup cream cheese (softened)

- 1/4 cup white chocolate chips

- 3 tbsp powdered sugar

CHOCOLATE GANACHE FROSTING

- 1/3 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips

- 1/2 cup whipping cream

- 1 tsp instant coffee granules

- 2 tbsp cocoa powder

- 1 tsp salt

CHOCOLATE GANACHE

- 1/3 cup semi sweet chocolate chips

- 2 tbsp whipping cream

COFFEE SOAK

- 1 cup of coffee

- 1 tbsp sugar

PREPARATION

BAKE THE CAKE

Bring all ingredients to room temperature before you begin. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit. Prepare one sheet pan with parchment paper lining the bottom.

To a large mixing bowl add the sugar, eggs and vegetable oil. Using a whisk cream together the ingredients until the mixture has no lumps. Slowly add the milk and vanilla. Whisking to bring the mixture together.

Into a separate large bowl add all the dry ingredients: flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda and salt. Mix to combine.

Brew one cup of strong coffee (or add 3 tsp of instant coffee to one cup of boiling water).

Alternate adding dry ingredients and the coffee, starting with flour and ending with coffee. Use a rubber spatula to continuously mix, just incorporating the flour. Do not over mix the batter because we do not want to develop too much gluten in the cake.

Spread evenly into the pan and bake for 15 minutes. Using a toothpick check if the cake is ready. If a toothpick inserted is clean or as a few crumbs it has baked. Cool the cake for 30 minutes in the pan.

Remove the parchment and cut into desired layers. Wrap the layers in plastic wrap and tin foil. Freeze until ready to use. 24 hours before you will be decorating the cake, refrigerate the layers to thaw.

WHITE CHOCOLATE FROSTING

Using a whisk or stand mixer whip the cream cheese, powdered sugar and whipping cream for two minutes.

Melt the white chocolate in a double boiler. Allow the chocolate to come to room temperature. Fold in the white chocolate to the whipped cream cheese frosting.

CHOCOLATE GANACHE FROSTING

In a small sauce pan heat up the whipping cream just until small bubbles start to appear. We are not looking for a boil.

Add the hot cream to the chocolate and let it sit for a couple minutes. Whisk the cream and chocolate into a glossy mixture. Add cocoa powder, salt and coffee granules. Chill the fridge for least 20 minutes.

Add the chilled cream to a stand mixer and whip until stiff peaks. Store in the fridge until ready to use.

CHOCOLATE GANACHE

In a small sauce pan heat up the whipping cream just until small bubbles start to appear. We are not looking for a boil.

Add the hot cream to the chocolate and let it sit for a couple minutes. Whisk the cream and chocolate into a glossy mixture. Use the ganache at room temperature.

COFFEE SOAK

Brew one cup of coffee, add 1 tbsp sugar, stir to combine. Chill the coffee.

ASSEMBLY

To a cake board, add one tbsp of frosting to secure the cake in place. Put down the first layer of cake. This should be the most stable layer.

Using a pastry brush soak the first layer with the coffee soak. Fill the chocolate mousse and white chocolate frosting in separate pastry bags or ziplock bags.

Pipe a thick layer of white chocolate cream cheese frosting on the first layer. Use an off-set spatula to smooth out the frosting. Add the next layer of cake. Soak and fill with chocolate mousse. Top the cake with the final cake layer. Add this layer flipped. The smoothest side should be on top.

Place the cake in the refrigerator for at least 10 minutes. Pour the ganache on top of the cake. Store the cake in the refrigerator. This cake is best eaten on the day of assembly.
