The Ultimate Chocolate Chip Cookie

Chewy, chocolatey center with thin and crisp edges. A lovely mid-day snack or fancy dessert with just some vanilla ice cream over top! Can you tell I love a good chocolate chip cookie? This dessert hits all the right spots with browned butter and tons of chocolate chips.

Makes 8 Large Cookies

INGREDIENTS

- 1 cup all purpose flour

- 1/2 cup unsalted butter

- 3/4 cup brown sugar

- 1/8 cup white sugar

- 1/2 to 3/4 cup chocolate chips

- 1 tsp vanilla extract (sub 1/2 tsp almond extract)

- 1/2 tsp baking soda

- 1 tsp salt (+ more to sprinkle on top)

PREPARATION

Preheat the oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit.

Take 1/4 of the butter and melt over a stove top on medium heat. Stir occassionally for 4 to 5 minutes until browned butter solids appear at the bottom of the pan. Allow for the butter to cool. Add in the remaining 1/4 cup butter in cubes into the melted butter. Stir to combine.

Into a large mixing bowl add in the cooled butter and all the sugar. Whisk for 3 to 5 minutes, until the mixture has combined. Add one egg and vanilla extract. Whisk together for around 3 minutes.

Add in flour, salt and baking soda. Use a rubber spatula to stir, just until flour has combined. Add in chocolate chips Use a rubber spatula to stir, just until combined.

Chill cookie dough in the refrigerator for atleast 15 minutes before baking. Using an ice cream scoop, scoop out the dough and place on to a lined baking sheet. Do not over crowd the pan.

Bake for 12 to 14 minutes or until golden brown. Sprinkle up to 1 additional tsp of salt on top of the hot cookies. Allow to cool for atleast 15 minutes in the pan out of the oven.
